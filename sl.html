Sri Lanka, officially known as the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, is an island nation located in South Asia, situated in the Indian Ocean to the southwest of the Bay of Bengal and to the southeast of the Arabian Sea. It is separated from the Indian subcontinent by the Gulf of Mannar and the Palk Strait. The capital and largest city of Sri Lanka is Colombo. Sri Lanka has a rich history that dates back to ancient times, with influences from various cultures including Indian, Arab, and European. The country is known for its diverse landscapes, which include lush tropical forests, picturesque beaches, and mountainous regions. The central highlands boast tea plantations, contributing to Sri Lanka's reputation as a major tea exporter. The majority of the population practices Buddhism, which has played a significant role in shaping the country's culture and traditions. Other religions, such as Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity, are also practiced by various communities. Despite periods of internal conflict, Sri Lanka has made strides in economic development and has a growing tourism industry, attracting visitors with its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality.
